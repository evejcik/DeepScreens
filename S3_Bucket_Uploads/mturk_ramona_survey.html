VISIBLE (Category 1)

Joint center is in-frame AND you can identify its location to within ±10 pixels based on direct visual evidence (edge, contour, color discontinuity, or anatomical landmark).

OCCLUDED (Category 2)

An identifiable occluder is blocking direct observation of the joint (body part, clothing, external object, another character, or atmospheric effect).

OFF-SCREEN (Category 3)

Joint center is outside the frame boundaries. Completely outside the video frame.

AMBIGUOUS (Category 4)

You genuinely cannot determine the visibility status due to unclear visual evidence, pitch-dark shadows, extreme motion blur, or other factors. Always confidence 1.

Occlusion Severity (if Occluded)

0: 0-25% occluded
1: 25-50% occluded
2: 50-75% occluded
3: 75-100% occluded

Confidence (1-5): How certain are you the visibility category classification is CORRECT?

5 - Certain: No doubt. The classification is unambiguous.

4 - Fairly Sure: High confidence. Visual evidence strongly supports this classification.

3 - Moderate: Reasonably confident but some uncertainty. Visual evidence supports classification but conditions aren't perfect.

2 - Unsure: Low confidence. Visual evidence is minimal or conditions are poor, but you're still committing to a classification.

1 - Guessing: Very low confidence. You're barely confident in your classification.

NOT about occlusion reason

You can be confident a joint is Occluded (confidence 5) but uncertain whether it's self_occlusion vs external_object (reason is secondary). The classification itself is certain; only the reason is unclear.

NOT about occlusion severity

You can be confident a joint is Occluded (confidence 5) but unsure if it's 25-50% or 50-75% occluded (severity is secondary). The visibility category is certain; only the degree is uncertain.

NOT about image quality alone

Poor lighting/resolution doesn't automatically mean low confidence IF you have clear visual landmarks. Low-res image with visible knee outline = Visible with moderate-to-high confidence.

In Summary:

Confidence measures: "Am I certain about this visibility classification?"

NOT: "Am I certain about the occlusion reason?" or "Am I certain about the occlusion severity?" or "Is the image quality good?"
